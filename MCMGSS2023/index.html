Abstracts

  • High dimensional convex hypersurfaces and their contact homologies (Russell Avdek)

    Recently there's been a number of breakthroughs in dim>3 contact topology around convex hypersurfaces (mostly from a geometric topology point of view). The course will give a friendly way to think about convex hypersurfaces and explain how to compute their SFT invariants. It will cover some homological algebra, explicit examples, and get into analytical details in later lectures. I'll assume familiarity with contact manifolds and their fillings as well as the basics of SFT (holomorphic curves and DGAs).

  • Geometry of holomorphic isometric embeddings between bounded symmetric domains and applications (Shan Tai Chan)

    In the first lecture, we first discuss some fundamental properties of bounded symmetric domains and their Bergman metrics. I will also explain some results of holomorphic isometric embeddings between bounded symmetric domains with respect to the Bergman metrics up to scalar constants.

    In the second lecture, I will continue to talk about more results of holomorphic isometric embeddings between bounded symmetric domains. After that, I will explain some results about the asymptotic behavior of local holomorphic curves exiting the boundary of a bounded symmetric domain and their applications based on my joint work with Ngaiming Mok. One of the applications is related to the hyperbolic Ax-Lindemann-Weierstrass conjecture that has been solved by Klingler-Ullmo-Yafaev (2016) for pure Shimura varieties.
